Our Heritage — Est. 1978

Born in the Valleys
of Kashmir

Since 1978, Kashmiré has been manufacturing exclusively shawls — the large, rectangular or square pieces of fabric central to Indian culture. We make nothing else, because we believe in mastering one craft completely.

Every shawl that leaves our workshop is a singular object — not a product, but a piece of living heritage. From raw fibre selection in the Himalayas to the final stitch, our artisans honour a tradition that predates industry.

Our Craftsmanship
01
Raw Material
Fibre Selection & Sourcing

We hand-select raw Pashmina, cashmere, silk cocoons and Merino fleece from trusted highland sources — tracing every fibre to its origin before a single thread is spun.

02
Spinning
Hand Spinning on Charkha

Master spinners use traditional wooden charkhas to draw fibres into gossamer-thin yarns. The exact twist count determines each shawl’s drape, warmth and lustre.

03
Weaving
Handloom Pit Weaving

On traditional pit-looms, master weavers interlace warp and weft with extraordinary precision. A complex Kani shawl may take 12–18 months of unbroken concentration.

04
Finishing
Wash, Finish & 36-Point QC

Each shawl is washed in cold spring water, stretched on wooden frames and finished by hand. A 36-point quality inspection ensures every piece leaves in flawless condition.
